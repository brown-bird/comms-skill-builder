Proposing a significant change

You want to propose a meaningful change and a poorly received pitch could sink the idea.

Say this

"I've been noticing [problem] and I think it's creating real friction. Does that match what you're seeing, or am I missing context?"

"I have some thoughts on how we might address it — but before I go into them, what's your sense of why it keeps happening?"

Steps

  • Check that others share your read of the problem before presenting your solution
  • Share your proposal as a starting point, not a finished plan
  • Check for genuine alignment before treating it as agreed

Don't

  • Present a solution before confirming anyone else sees the problem
  • Frame the change as obviously necessary
  • Treat a positive reaction as full commitment
