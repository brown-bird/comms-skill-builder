Pitching an idea when the timing feels off

You have a good idea but the timing feels wrong — a flat reception could kill it.

Say this

"I've been thinking about [problem area] — I'm not sure if now is the right time to dig into it, but I wanted to flag it's on my mind. When would be a good moment?"

"I have something I'd like to bring to you, but I want to wait until the current crunch passes. Can we find 20 minutes next week?"

Steps

  • Test the water by surfacing the problem first, not the solution
  • Pick a low-stakes moment rather than a formal presentation
  • Name the bad timing directly and reserve time for when conditions improve

Don't

  • Force the pitch during organizational stress
  • Wait indefinitely for a perfect moment
  • Present a fully formed proposal when early input would build ownership
