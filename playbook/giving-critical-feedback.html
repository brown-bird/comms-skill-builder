Giving critical feedback to a peer

You need to give a peer feedback that isn't formally invited — the first challenge is creating conditions where it lands.

Say this

"Can I share something I've been sitting with? I want to flag it as a peer, not as a verdict."

"I noticed [specific behavior] in [specific context]. What was going on for you there?"

Steps

  • Check whether they're open to hearing it before delivering
  • Describe the specific behavior, not your interpretation of it
  • Name your relational intent when closing

Don't

  • Deliver feedback while still carrying frustration about it
  • Speak for others you haven't actually consulted
