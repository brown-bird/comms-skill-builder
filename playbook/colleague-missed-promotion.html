Playbook › A colleague missed a promotion — and you got it

Situation

Your colleague — someone you work with daily — just found out they didn't get promoted. You did. You'll need to keep working closely together, and the dynamic between you has shifted, at least temporarily. How you handle the next 24–48 hours matters more than you might expect.

Applicable Principles

Empathy & Tone Situational Awareness

What to Avoid

  • Celebrating visibly near them — even with other colleagues, it reads as insensitive
  • Avoiding them entirely — it signals guilt or discomfort and makes the gap worse
  • Over-apologizing or saying "I don't even know why they picked me" — this minimizes your achievement and feels hollow to them
  • Rushing to "normalize" things before they've had time to process
  • Making it about your relationship: "I hope this doesn't change things between us" puts the burden on them to reassure you

Recommended Approach

  • Find a private, low-pressure moment to acknowledge it briefly — don't let days pass in silence
  • Lead with recognition of their experience, not your own news
  • Keep it short — this is a check-in, not a long conversation
  • Don't offer unsolicited advice about what they should do next
  • Give them space after — let them set the pace for returning to normal

Example Language

"I heard the news and I didn't want to just pretend it didn't happen. I imagine that's really disappointing — you've put a lot into this. I'm not going to make a big thing of it, I just wanted to say I see it."

Follow-Up

Let them lead the return to normal working cadence. In the weeks ahead, be consistent and collaborative — don't overcorrect by being overly deferential, but stay attentive to moments when they might need space or acknowledgment. Over time, how you show up as a peer matters more than anything you say in the immediate aftermath.
