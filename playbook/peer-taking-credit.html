Playbook › A peer is taking credit for your work

Situation

A colleague has been taking credit for work you contributed to — in emails, in meetings, in front of leadership. It's happened more than once. You're not sure if it's intentional, but the pattern is clear enough that staying silent is costing you visibility and starting to affect your trust in the relationship.

Example Language

"I wanted to flag something that's been bothering me. In [specific meeting/email], [my contribution wasn't mentioned / the work was presented as yours]. That's happened a few times now, and I'd rather sort it out between us than let it sit. Going forward, I'd like us to be deliberate about attributing work accurately when we present to the broader team."

Principles

  • Address it privately, close in time to a specific incident while it's still concrete
  • Describe the pattern with a specific example rather than a sweeping accusation
  • Name the impact — on your visibility, your motivation, your trust in the relationship
  • Make a specific, forward-looking ask: what you want attribution to look like going forward
  • Give them a chance to respond — there may be a genuine perception gap worth understanding

Anti-patterns

  • Calling them out in front of others — public confrontation rarely lands well and makes repair harder
  • Staying silent and hoping it stops — it usually doesn't
  • Complaining to mutual colleagues before addressing it directly — it poisons the well and gets back to them
  • Framing it as accusatory: "you've been stealing my credit" — even if true, it triggers defensiveness
  • Waiting so long that you arrive at the conversation already frustrated, which makes the tone hard to control

Applicable Principles

Conflict Resolution Clarity & Brevity Situational Awareness

Follow-Up

If attribution improves, acknowledge it — briefly and genuinely. If the pattern continues after a direct conversation, escalate with specifics: document particular incidents and bring them to your manager as a concrete pattern, not a vague complaint.
