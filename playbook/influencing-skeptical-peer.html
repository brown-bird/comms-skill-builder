Getting a skeptical peer to support a process change

You need buy-in from a peer who has no reason to say yes — the change creates extra work for their team.

Say this

"I know this adds work on your side, and I don't want to pretend otherwise. Can you help me understand what your biggest concerns are? I'd rather solve for those now than hand you something finished."

Steps

  • Start with genuine curiosity about their concerns
  • Acknowledge the cost to their team explicitly
  • Invite them into the design with a draft, not a final plan

Don't

  • Present the change as a done deal
  • Escalate to their manager before trying peer-level influence
  • Treat their skepticism as an obstacle rather than information
