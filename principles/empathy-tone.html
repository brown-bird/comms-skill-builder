Principle 01

Empathy & Tone

Adjust your approach based on the emotional context of the situation before focusing on content or outcome.

What it is

Empathy in communication isn't about agreeing or feeling what someone else feels — it's about demonstrating that you understand their experience before moving forward. Tone is the signal that carries that understanding (or undermines it). The same words delivered with different tone produce entirely different results.

People generally cannot hear your message until they feel heard themselves. Empathy creates the conditions for real communication to happen.

When to apply it

Someone is visibly upset or stressed Delivering difficult news After a conflict or rupture Someone just experienced a setback High-stakes feedback conversations Cross-cultural or unfamiliar contexts

Key behaviors

  • Name or acknowledge the other person's emotional state before moving to problem-solving
  • Match your pace and energy to theirs — slow down if they're distressed
  • Use open body language and genuine eye contact
  • Ask what they need before assuming: "Do you want to talk through it, or just have me listen?"
  • Avoid minimizing language: "I know exactly how you feel," "it could be worse," "at least…"
  • Validate before advising — even if you disagree with how they're feeling

What undermines it

  • Jumping to solutions before the person feels understood
  • Letting your tone contradict your words (calm content + impatient delivery)
  • Using empathy as a tactic rather than a genuine attempt to understand
  • Over-apologizing or over-mirroring — it reads as performative
