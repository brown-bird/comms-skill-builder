Principle 03

Conflict Resolution

De-escalate tension and move toward shared understanding — without requiring either side to simply capitulate.

What it is

Conflict is rarely about the surface issue. It's usually about unmet needs, bruised trust, or a difference in values. Effective resolution means addressing the underlying dynamic, not just reaching a temporary ceasefire.

The goal isn't to win or to smooth things over — it's to restore a functional relationship and reach an outcome both parties can genuinely commit to.

When to apply it

Recurring friction with a colleague or family member After a specific incident or blowup When a relationship feels stuck or tense Disagreement that's escalating in tone Mediating between others

Key behaviors

  • Choose timing deliberately — don't initiate conflict conversations when either party is activated
  • Separate the person from the problem; attack the issue, not the individual
  • Describe observable behavior, not inferred intent: "You sent that email without looping me in" not "You went behind my back"
  • Acknowledge your own contribution to the dynamic, even if it's small
  • Ask about their perspective before defending yours
  • Look for the need underneath the position — what do they actually want?

What undermines it

  • Raising the issue when you're still emotionally flooded
  • Listing grievances instead of focusing on one issue at a time
  • Using absolutes: "you always," "you never"
  • Conceding just to end the discomfort — without genuine resolution
  • Bringing in past conflicts to score points in the current one
