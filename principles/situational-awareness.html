Principle 04 · Situational Awareness

Adapt to the situation.

What it is

Situational awareness is the ability to read the context around a conversation — the mood, the stakes, the power dynamics, the history, the timing — and calibrate your approach accordingly. It's the meta-skill that helps you decide which other principle to lean on.

When to apply it

Entering a new team or relationship Before a high-stakes conversation When a conversation takes an unexpected turn In group settings with shifting dynamics When you're unsure how your approach is landing During organizational change or uncertainty

Key behaviors

  • Before any important conversation, ask: What's going on for this person right now?
  • Notice what's not being said — hesitation, topic avoidance, changes in tone or energy
  • Track the energy in the room: who defers to whom, who's checked out, who's uncomfortable
  • Consider timing: is this the right moment for this conversation?
  • Adjust your formality, pace, and vocabulary to fit the context
  • Check your assumptions about what the other person wants from this exchange

What undermines it

  • Treating every situation with the same default approach
  • Focusing so much on your own message that you miss signals from the other person
  • Ignoring context you find inconvenient ("Now's not a great time but I need to say this")
  • Acting on your first read without checking whether it still holds

Playbook entries

  • Pitching an idea when the timing feels off
  • A colleague missed a promotion — and you got it
  • Escalating a problem to a senior stakeholder
  • A peer is taking credit for your work
  • Proposing a significant change
